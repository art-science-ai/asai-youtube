Book deals are hard to come by. It helps if you have an existing audience of thousands of people who are near-guaranteed to buy your book. If not? Buddy, you‚Äôd better start posting. This is why Caitlin Murray started her Instagram account, [@bigtimeadulting](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.H8ioGyKsbhfyH8kTy6TPswv4c6Ly0rhatOwzEa9-qFKvwZIRnBjwQYDu-EDotWy59ni7zekiq6iROohEXUf0OGF5uikifRAsIyYya3J9SfN8C9ZLuLD9T4nCyzS2SsZyDoudyKGo38VKCkHZQqaBo0cqTTyqlABC9iYs58hdFFwzhS5I8vCdm%5Floq2KXS%5FVfszwQrMBNJcvVKCCZgllLyrR8r0lmWYIEc3khvoyWvxM/470/YLmw6atASeamWcDddQv3vQ/h6/h001.HIhyzd3pU123mf6WUWCO34pF0uk-zusPLHetmc-P8C4), at the end of 2018\. 

Caitlin‚Äôs first child, Calum, turned three in November 2016 and was diagnosed with leukemia one month later. As a parent, as a mother, what do you do? Obviously, you take your kid to the hospital secure the best possible treatment, but what do _you_ do?

Caitlin‚Äôs answer was to write. She started emailing friends and family regular updates about Calum‚Äôs health before switching to blogging, for posterity‚Äôs sake. Happily, it took less than four years for Calum to beat the cancer. 

In the middle of Calum‚Äôs fight with leukemia, an editor Caitlin knew asked if she‚Äôd be interested in writing a book. Caitlin‚Äôs blogging was an important creative outlet in a tough time ‚Äî yes, she wanted to write a book. A publisher squashed her dream: ‚ÄúThe writing is fine, but she doesn‚Äôt have any following,‚Äù as Caitlin paraphrased. ‚ÄúSo I was like, well, maybe I‚Äôll start an Instagram page.‚Äù

‚ÄúAt that point, I was still working. I was pregnant with my third child. I had a five-year-old who was in treatment for leukemia and a three-year-old. And I just, you know, I had no fucks left to give. So, I came to Instagram and started sharing my inner thoughts on motherhood and the challenges of everyday life as a mom.  
  
Not so much to do with the deeper, more challenging things I was facing at that time with my son, but **it just gave me this opportunity to be free and honest about all the hard parts of being a mom.**‚Äù

Six years, over 1,000 posts, and countless Stories, later the @bigtimeadulting account has 1.4 million followers. 

By the end of her first year, Caitlin had 31k followers. **The real inflection point comes in October 2022,** nearly three years after starting the account. Instagram launched Reels, she started experimenting with them, and new followers came flooding in. That month Caitlin had 141k followers ‚Äî one year later, October 2023, she had 1.13M.

Three years from 0 to 141k. One year to add 1 million on top.

Outside of regular posting, Caitlin was highly active on stories and ‚Äî crucially ‚Äî made sure to connect with like-minded accounts and comment on their posts.

Whenever a friend, "a like-minded account," shared one of Caitlin's posts, she'd notice a follower bump. When an algorithm-driven social media platform releases a new feature, like Reels, the company tends to tweak the algorithm to promote content that takes advantage of the new feature. In other words, Caitlin grew because she was early to Reels, and Instagram was promoting Reels.

**But the algorithm only magnified Caitlin herself**. She‚Äôs charismatic and already had over 100k invested subscribers at the time ‚Äî she‚Äôd been developing the @bigtimeadulting voice for nearly four years. The fire was already burning; Reels just bloomed it in the wind.
