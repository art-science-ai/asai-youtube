## Acquiring _TWJN_ and making it a better business 

It‚Äôs early 2021\. Toronto is in the twin grips of winter and pandemic; Akhil Chauhan has too much time on his hands. He‚Äôs just received a promotion at his day job, but he craves a creative outlet independent of that job to push and challenge him ‚Äî maybe even to make a little extra money from.

Akhil maps out ‚Äúsix or eight potential avenues.‚Äù He could start an app. Maybe just pick up a new hobby? Or start a media business. He‚Äôs been reading about all these writers starting newsletters ‚Äî maybe a newsletter could scratch his itch. He‚Äôs always enjoyed writing, though he‚Äôs never done it professionally.

Choosing what topic to build a newsletter around, how to give it a unique angle, then attracting those first thousand subscribers isn‚Äôt always straightforward. Akhil wanted to skip the rigamarole:

‚ÄúI thought, maybe I could save two years worth of grind and trying to find a niche and get into something where a lot of the market validation essentially has already been done. And I could find something that maybe I could just improve, innovate, and sort of grow from there.‚Äú

**In other words, Akhil wanted to cut from the back of the line (a general desire to write a newsletter) and walk directly into the club (purchase a newsletter with proven product-market fit).** Still ‚Äî which newsletter in what niche? He considered a few AI newsletters, though this was still almost a year before ChatGPT launched and AI‚Äôs cultural stock went through the roof.

In the end, Akhil bought [_TWJN_](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.NgPAn2DAGv3NuJuihremPFFQLobwD1nUmbNmMgSMHYiJYVVOJYa93jgN4cVzGiDnN-HFAHQzuU8rrurtrvWiTNbV9qndkCzWvsAW3Oy2H73cJbMiXZkxVATCkmO02CLrvwXziZwhEkCewFcspziGF4TV-P5jDOv7xq3hC0FNp5kOTT5Na6p4eXY0IWv8qzrPmkaC%5F1UzglHRcBmmYNJBDw/47e/toD7a7hRQR2vs%5FXvYyuuEQ/h9/h001.SJ7BnG%5F78riSOWZinLVIuYS%5FS7gkA9iUz58DA22YSg0)because it was most aligned with his interests and price range:

‚ÄúI've always been interested in writing, I've always cared about good journalism. And here's this thing that can help writers with their jobs. And it can also help me do some of my own writing in a structured and formatted way.‚Äù

The original creator had listed _TWJN_ on an online marketplace, but he wasn‚Äôt quite ready to sell. They talked it out and Akhil convinced the seller he was the right person to steward the project; they negotiated a price and closed the deal.

The market has changed, and benchmarks are more established. It‚Äôs more common now to negotiate based on price-per-subscriber, add a multiplier, add a premium; their pricing discussions took a more holistic look at the project.

## A clear mission is a true compass

**Akhil began operating** _**TWJN**_ **in March 2022, and combing through the archives, I couldn‚Äôt determine exactly which issue was his first.** In the two years since, of course, I‚Äôve noticed incremental changes ‚Äî rebranding here and there, telltale signs of migration (from Mailchimp to Subkit to beehiiv), the addition of a second, premium-only weekly email with extra opportunities. ‚ÄúThe mission from the start has always been to help freelance writers find better-paying jobs. And that's really unchanged. I took over in about March 2022 and since then have just been doubling down on helping people find better paying jobs.‚Äù

**Ultimately,** [_**TWJN**_](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.NgPAn2DAGv3NuJuihremPFFQLobwD1nUmbNmMgSMHYiJYVVOJYa93jgN4cVzGiDnN-HFAHQzuU8rrurtrvWiTNbV9qndkCzWvsAW3Oy2H73cJbMiXZkxVATCkmO02CLrvwXziZwhEkCewFcspziGF4TV-P5jDOv7xq3hC0FNp5kOTT5Na6p4eXY0IWv8qzrPmkaC%5F1UzglHRcBmmYNJBDw/47e/toD7a7hRQR2vs%5FXvYyuuEQ/h11/h001.F8-f3rohQBKGyOKI0%5Fm4%5FhuUAZXYRNPvx8T6CmU43H8) **is a service, and Akhil knows to stay out of the way.** It‚Äôs not The Akhil Newsletter. Sure, he shares little notes and anecdotes in the intro each week, but it‚Äôs about the writers who open the newsletter each week and select a few opportunities to pitch.

‚ÄúI got an email once from a writer who was really struggling and looking to break into the B2B tech writing space, but she hadn't had much experience in that. **All newsletters have a purpose.** Few are as service-oriented and to the point as _TWJN_‚Äôs ‚Äî I admire the clarity. Some newsletters‚Äô purpose is to give the creator a creative outlet. ## Listing subscriber numbers at the top of every newsletter

You‚Äôve probably seen this before: open up a newsletter, and right at the top, it says one of two things:

* ‚ÄúHello ‚Äî and welcome to the 137 new readers who‚Äôve joined us since last week!‚Äù
* ‚ÄúHappy Sunday to 2,981 motivated writers‚Äù

**These are two slightly different ways of saying the same thing: look, reader, at how many people besides you read this newsletter ‚Äî it must be good.**

I made that first one up based on newsletters I‚Äôve read in the past, but the second one is straight from _TWJN_ issue #64\. Sent in April 2022, it was one of Akhil‚Äôs first few issues post-acquisition. He started every single issue with this same message, number updated, until he suddenly stopped after issue #147: ‚ÄúHappy Sunday to 8,237 motivated writers.‚Äù

**I asked Akhil why he started including these numbers then stopped 1.5 years later:**

‚ÄúI felt like it was more of a vanity metric. It just made me feel good when I put it at the top of the newsletter, because I'd look at the issues week in, week out, and it's like, yeah, look, it's gone up.

But I thought of this from the perspective of the subscriber: they don't care how many people subscribe. They want to know how many opportunities there are. It felt like that was something for me and it wasn't something for them.‚Äù

**Broadly, I‚Äôm not sure he‚Äôs right. Nor am I sure he‚Äôs wrong.** Given that he‚Äôs sharing a list of job opportunities, the number here serves the same purpose as the number on a LinkedIn job listing (i.e, 94 applicants). It gives readers a sense of urgency ‚Äî if I‚Äôm a writer reading that 8,237 other writers are reading _TWJN_, I‚Äôm thinking I‚Äôd better send out some pitches ASAP. 

For newsletters generally, though, I do think sharing subscriber numbers at the start of every issue can help as a form of social proof ‚Äî there must be something worthwhile here if all these people subscribe, right? 

On the other hand, maybe it‚Äôs alienating! Maybe you want readers to feel a more personal connection to the newsletter ‚Äî not to know they‚Äôre one of thousands reading the exact same email.

I can see good arguments for and against consistently disclosing subscriber numbers. It‚Äôs really dependent on context. **Do you feel strongly either way? Reply and let me know.**
