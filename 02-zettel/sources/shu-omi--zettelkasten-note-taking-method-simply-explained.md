# AI notes

## TLDR
- Zettelkasten is a knowledge management system developed by Niklas Luhmann that uses index cards to build a web of interconnected ideas.
- The method involves taking literature notes, bibliographic notes, and permanent notes to create meaningful connections between concepts.
- By focusing on retrieval rather than storage, Zettelkasten enables efficient idea generation and writing.
- The system requires writing notes in your own words to ensure deep understanding.
- Over time, it builds a personal knowledge base that facilitates creative output and argumentation.

## Key ideas
- Zettelkasten ("slip box" in German) allows building a huge web of knowledge through interconnected notes.
- Literature notes capture key information from sources in your own words, while bibliographic notes record source details.
- Permanent notes synthesize new ideas by finding connections between existing knowledge and new information.
- Keywords should be chosen based on retrieval needs, not categorization.
- Fleeting notes capture spontaneous ideas that can be developed into permanent notes.

## Detailed Summary
- Introduction to Zettelkasten: Developed by Niklas Luhmann, a prolific sociologist who published 58 books and hundreds of articles using this system.
- Core components: Two boxes - one for bibliographic notes, one for permanent notes; uses index cards and pen.
- Step 1 - Literature notes: While reading, write brief notes in your own words on index cards about information you want to remember.
- Step 2 - Bibliographic notes: After finishing a source, record reference details and brief content summary on one side of a card.
- Step 3 - Permanent notes: Review previous notes, find connections to existing knowledge, and create new notes that add value to your thinking.
- Finding connections: Ask how new ideas relate to what you already know, what they mean for other concepts, and how they can explain other things.
- Keyword assignment: Choose keywords based on when you'd want to rediscover the note, not for categorization.
- Linking notes: Place new permanent notes behind relevant existing notes, add links, and index for easy discovery.
- Fleeting notes: Capture spontaneous ideas in a notebook, review within days, and develop into permanent notes if valuable.
- Writing process: Once you have a knowledge web, identify gaps, read more, then reassemble notes into coherent writing without starting from scratch.
