## Summary

- **Do not consent to routine episiotomy.** All major guidelines recommend **restrictive/selective use only**. WHO 2018, ACOG 2018 PB198, RCOG/NICE, FOGSI 2024 GCPR.
- If an episiotomy is **truly indicated**, ask for a **mediolateral cut ~60° from the midline** at crowning (avoid midline). RCOG OASI Care Bundle, NICE MTG47, StatPearls.
- **Prevention bundle**: warm perineal compresses + manual support at crowning + antenatal perineal massage from ~35 weeks (1–2×/week). Cochrane 2017, Cochrane 2022, RACGP.

---

## What the guidelines say

- **WHO (2018)** — “Routine or liberal use of episiotomy is **not recommended** for spontaneous vaginal birth.” link.
    
- **ACOG PB 198 (2018)** — Routine episiotomy **offers no maternal benefit** vs restrictive use; may increase anal incontinence; use **restrictively**. link.
    
- **RCOG/NICE (UK)** — Avoid routine episiotomy; **if indicated**, perform **mediolateral at ~60°** at crowning; consider with **forceps/ventouse** to reduce OASI risk. RCOG OASI, NICE MTG47.
    
- **FOGSI (India, 2024)** — **Discourages routine episiotomy**; recommends **selective/judicious use** and prevention techniques. link.
    

## What the research shows (why “restrictive”)

- **Cochrane review (2017, 11 RCTs; ~6,000 women):** Selective episiotomy led to **fewer severe perineal/vaginal traumas** vs routine use; **no neonatal benefit** to routine use. [clinical summary](https://www.cochrane.org/evidence/CD000081_selective-versus-routine-use-episiotomy-vaginal-birth), full text.
    
- **Technique matters:** **Midline** episiotomy increases OASI; **mediolateral ~60°** is protective, especially with instruments. StatPearls, RCOG OASI, NICE MTG47.
    
- **Adjuncts that help:** Warm compresses and hands-on support reduce third-/fourth‑degree tears; antenatal perineal massage reduces need for episiotomy and persistent pain. Cochrane 2017, Cochrane 2022.
    

## When an episiotomy can be appropriate (selective use)

Consider only with **clear clinical indications**, e.g.:

- **Instrument‑assisted birth** (forceps/ventouse), particularly in first births. RCOG OASI.
    
- **Expedited birth** needed (e.g., fetal heart concerns) where a cut is expected to **materially shorten** delivery. ACOG PB198.
    
- **Imminent severe tear** judged clinically (ischaemic, non‑stretching perineum). RCOG OASI.
    

**If performed:** request **mediolateral ~60°** at crowning (right‑sided cut), adequate local anaesthesia, and careful layered repair. RCOG OASI, NICE MTG47.

## India‑specific context

- **FOGSI (2024) GCPR** aligns with WHO/ACOG/UK: **no routine episiotomy**; emphasize prevention and proper technique. link.
    
- **Angle accuracy matters:** Survey of Indian obstetricians highlights difficulty achieving true **~60°** without guides/training—supporting a _do‑it‑well‑when‑indicated_ approach. study. Tools like **Episcissors‑60** can help standardize angle where available. NICE MTG47.
    

## Discussion plan with your OB (copy/paste)

- **Policy:** “We **do not consent to routine episiotomy**; please use it **only if clearly indicated**, consistent with WHO/FOGSI/ACOG/RCOG.” (WHO, FOGSI).
    
- **Numbers:** “What are your unit’s **episiotomy rate** (for spontaneous births) and **OASI rate**?” (quality metrics)
    
- **Technique:** “If needed, do you perform **mediolateral ~60°** (not midline)? Do you use angle guides or training?” (RCOG OASI, NICE MTG47).
    
- **Prevention bundle:** “Can we plan for **warm compresses + hands‑on perineal support** at crowning, and I’ll start **perineal massage from 35 weeks (1–2×/wk)**?” (Cochrane 2017, Cochrane 2022/RACGP).
    

**Rapid consent sentence for labour:**

> _“We don’t consent to a routine episiotomy. If you believe one is_ **_clinically necessary_**_, please say: ‘We recommend an episiotomy now because ___. We’ll perform a_ **_mediolateral ~60°_** _cut.’ We’ll consent if we agree.”_
