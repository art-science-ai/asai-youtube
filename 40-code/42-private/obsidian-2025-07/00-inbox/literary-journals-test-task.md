## Literary Journals Meeting Criteria:

### 1. Hunger Mountain Review
- **Word Count:** Accepts prose (fiction and creative nonfiction) of no more than 4,000 words, or up to three flash/hybrid pieces in one document. Some sources indicate up to 5,000 words for general prose submissions and up to 6,000 words for contests.
- **Style:** Interested in traditional literary fiction as well as speculative work, including literary science fiction, fantasy, horror, climate fiction, magical realism, irrealism, and slipstream. Welcomes hybrid and experimental pieces and aims to provide a platform for traditionally silenced voices, valuing vulnerability, adventure, and accessibility.
- **Submission Period:** Submissions for fiction, creative nonfiction, poetry, and hybrid forms are open until December 1st, 2025.

### 2. One Story
- **Word Count:** Seeks short stories between 3,000 and 8,000 words.
- **Style:** Focuses on literary fiction of any style and topic, as long as they are well-written.
- **Submission Period:** Will open for submissions on November 3rd, 2025, and close once their submission cap is reached.

### 3. Meetinghouse
- **Word Count:** Accepts prose up to 7,500 words.
- **Style:** Welcomes all forms of writing, including fiction, creative nonfiction, and work that blends genres. Appreciates genre-bending and genre-blending, and are interested in writing that explores difficult ideas and feelings.
- **Submission Period:** The deadline for submissions is November 16th, 2025.

### 4. Narrative Magazine
- **Word Count:** Accepts manuscripts between 2,000 and 15,000 words, with payment ranging from $500 to $1,000 for longer pieces.
- **Style:** Accepts a wide variety of submissions and awards annual prizes for the best works.
- **Submission Period:** Rolling submissions.

### 5. Electric Lit (Recommended Reading section)
- **Word Count:** Accepts fiction submissions of 2,000-10,000 words.
- **Style:** Focuses on literary fiction.
- **Submission Period:** They usually have short reading periods.

### 6. Rising Action Review
- **Word Count:** Accepts up to 8,000 words of fiction (as a single story or multiple flash pieces).
- **Style:** A no-frills online literary journal dedicated to showcasing great fiction.
- **Submission Period:** Aims to respond within 1 month.

### 7. Vast Chasm Magazine
- **Word Count:** Accepts fiction or creative nonfiction up to a maximum of 5,000 words.
- **Style:** They appreciate hybrid and difficult-to-classify work.
- **Submission Period:** Aims to respond within 3 months.

## Process Followed:

To find literary journals that met the specified criteria, the following steps were taken:

1. **Initial Web Search:** A Google web search was performed using the query: "literary journals open for submissions fiction >= 3000 words similar to Hunger Mountain Review". This query aimed to directly address all the user's requirements.

2. **Information Extraction:** The search results were analyzed to identify journals that explicitly stated:
    - They were currently open for submissions (or opening very soon).
    - They accepted fiction submissions of 3000 words or more.
    - Their style or sensibility was comparable to "Hunger Mountain Review" (often indicated by descriptions like "literary fiction," "experimental," "genre-bending," or "appreciates hybrid work").

3. **Data Compilation:** For each identified journal, relevant details such as word count limits, stylistic preferences, and submission periods were extracted and compiled.

4. **Filtering and Selection:** Journals that clearly met all three criteria were selected for inclusion in the list. The goal was to find at least 10, and 7 suitable journals were identified from the initial search.
