	 - List of emotional intelligence competencies
		 - Self-Awareness
			 - Aware of my emotions and feelings moment-to-moment and of how they influence my behavior. Aware of when I am "emotionally flooded" (i.e., lose my cool) in a situation.

			 - Aware of when I become critical, defensive, contemptuous, or shut down.

			 - Aware of which emotions I show, and the influence my words and nonverbals have on others. Aware of my mindful choosing vs. impulsive reacting.

		 - Self-Regulation
			 - Able to communicate basic emotions. Able to align intensity and expression with intentions. Able to soothe myself when I have strong emotions. Able to recognize and manage my moods.

			 - Able to suspend judgments and negative attributions, especially when others have strong emotions.

			 - Comfortable with ambiguity and change. Able to suspend judgment while seeking to understand. Confident to demonstrate my abilities. Accepting and open to feedback to candidly assess them.

		 - Motivation
			 - Self-directing and self-motivating with realistic and healthy interpersonal expectations and goals.

			 - Willing to take risks to improve my skills. Able to manage the uncomfortable feelings which risk- taking may trigger.

			 - Willing to give others authentic appreciation and constructive feedback about their goal achievement.

			 - Able to recognize and disclose my feelings to show my motivation and engagement. Able to express my commitment to personal growth and change.

		 - Empathy
			 - Recognize another‚Äôs basic emotions, inquire empathically, and respond sensitively. Proactively and openly make appropriate acts of repair.

			 - Listen to and confirm content and emotions - such that the other person would acknowledge being heard and understood.

			 - Be considerate, open-minded, and inclusive of a diversity of life experiences and social identity (ethnicity, gender, culture, etc.)

			 - Respond sensitively to others' feelings, needs, and requests for help, even when communicated unclearly.

		 - Social Skill
			 - Able to influence a group so that my needs, concerns, and goals are included in the group's agenda, process, or mission.

			 - Constructively address interpersonal differences, even when I experience them as difficult or high-conflict.

			 - Able to give constructive feedback in a connecting manner, especially when I feel angry or resentful.

			 - Able to take different roles in support of a team (initiator, supporter, questioner, observer, etc.).

			 - Able to proactively disclose to others how they influence me, in a manner which builds trust and cooperation.
