			 - She talked about the Indian government's response to Covid in India. The good part was that India recognized it very early on, took it very seriously, adopted a scientific approach to Covid, and instituted a pretty rigorous lockdown. But the bad part was that the govt didn't really have a detailed level plan for the exact rules of the lockdown (e.g. who's allowed exceptions and for what â€” which led to some doctors getting beaten by the police for example), also they didn't manage to drastically incraase testing, PPE, and other such supplies, and lastly and perhaps most importantly they didn't have a plan in place for economic risk mitigation. Like what exactly were all the migrant workers and daily laborers supposed to do when they were told to stop workign and start quarantining? How do they survive? This was not addressed. Also apparently there's a lot of bureaucratic centralizing hangover from colonial era and from the socialist era. India has a strong history of centralization, and that's only starting to go away, but it will still take time.

			 - They also talked about why land owning sizes are so small in India today. This is something that [[Dad]] always talks about. Dad's explanation is more along the lines of intergenerational dwindling of resources (e.g. a father divides his plot of land between his two sons, who then further divide the same plot between their grandsons, etc.). But Shruti also talks about how this was a conscious policy adopted by the Indian government as a counterveiling force against feudalism, which was leading to the exploitation of farmers.  It's funny that now we've come to the other point of view, where we think that the greater good can be realized by having large plots of land that are adopting industrialized manufacturing methods.
