### _**üåé The (geo)politics of AI**_

In our previous edition, we looked at the tricky time AI companies have in navigating the Gulf. While local investors can write the multi-billion dollar cheques needed to fund expensive hardware, there is a web of complex institutional ties to China. G42, a sprawling Abu Dhabi-based AI company and investor, had rung a series of [alarm bells](https://substack.com/redirect/50b8a9ee-e834-4120-a24c-5dea6d7794d8?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) in Washington for being simultaneously an OpenAI partner and a shareholder in ByteDance and a series of other Chinese companies. Following US pressure, G42 [sold all of its stakes](https://substack.com/redirect/f4b98652-8ee2-4514-b230-41c7d5094443?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) in Chinese companies earlier this year. Now G42 has gone one step further, taking a [$1.5 billion investment](https://substack.com/redirect/d0ce0e4c-0476-4628-8b3a-33accb05a64f?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) from Microsoft - definitively picking sides.

This sequence of events illustrates how the AI arms race is making geopolitical divides simultaneously more fraught and simpler. In its quest to defend US companies‚Äô IP and prevent China from accessing the most advanced capabilities, the US government is gradually losing its tolerance for ambiguous partnership: you‚Äôre either with us or against us. 

On the one hand, this seems like bad news for Gulf nations like the UAE, who‚Äôve depicted themselves externally as a regional Switzerland, where anyone is free to make money provided they don‚Äôt ask too many questions. But, the fact that US officials [worked so closely](https://substack.com/redirect/55134629-b14d-4782-8dea-b6851c3ffbe9?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) for months to hammer out an agreement with Abu Dhabi‚Äôs leadership and G42 underlines the importance of having allies prepared to spend big on AI. Maybe the best place to be in an arms race is the middle - who knows, Brad Smith may well be rushing to join your board.

Back in greater Europe, spare a thought for Rishi Sunak. The leader of safety island‚Äôs early access agreement with frontier model providers appears to be [struggling with teething problems](https://substack.com/redirect/dc4ac69a-f66a-443e-8b1d-924c159205a5?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI). It turns out that corporations with highly valuable IP are much more willing to sign theoretical commitments to share their technology with the government than they are to ‚Ä¶ actually share it. Of the leading AI labs, so far, only Google DeepMind has allowed the UK‚Äôs AI Safety Institute to test a model before full public release. 

UK pre-deployment testing appears to be suffering from two main problems. 

Firstly, companies‚Äô concern that, in a highly competitive environment, the process will slow model releases. We‚Äôve already privately heard stories of entire releases being junked because a competitor has managed to exceed performance days before launch day. 

Secondly, the UK is not the US. Companies fear that by allowing one non-US country pre-deployment access, they‚Äôll open the floodgates for others to insist on the same. This is ultimately why ‚Äòleadership‚Äô on safety is challenging without accompanying leadership in capabilities. Even with the best qualified safety institute in the world, it will be a continual battle to assert relevance, especially as the US continues to [bulk out its own rival](https://substack.com/redirect/6bc44c43-1702-4e8e-98e7-d4b2f4f7ea88?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI).

The UK is down, but not out. The Competition and Markets Authority, the world-leading opponent of midcap tech M&A, dropped [its review](https://substack.com/redirect/30dc148d-a141-41e3-aef5-3e754f81e02a?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) of competition in the frontier model space. The report warned of the possibility that _‚Äúincumbent firms may try to use partnerships and investments to quash competitive threads, even where it is uncertain whether those threats will materalise‚Äù_. To combat this, it suggested a broadening of the scope of merger control rules to make it easier for regulators to intervene in non-traditional anti-competitive arrangements.

While a functional, competitive marketplace is important, with many of these interventions, it‚Äôs unclear what regulators believe this should look like beyond ‚Äúnot this‚Äù. As a result, many of the accompanying decisions seem arbitrary. For example, the EU is investigating Microsoft-Mistral but not Microsoft-OpenAI, while the CMA is investigating Anthropic-AWS but not Anthropic-Google. The FTC is at least being consistent in seemingly investigating everyone.

Ultimately, considering the constraints around hardware and capex, the alternative to this world of partnerships is consolidation. While arguably logical, competition regulators would undoubtedly dislike and seek to frustrate it at every turn. Short of artisanal model builders hammering together GPUs in their garden sheds or NVIDIA deciding to become a not-for-profit, partnerships are the next best thing. 

While regulators circle on competition, the generative AI copyright wars continue to rumble on. While the legal position remains as ambiguous as ever, the number of incidents and licensing deals continues to snowball. Ed Newton-Rex, composer turned Stability exec turned CEO of Fairly Trained, [put the boot](https://substack.com/redirect/772c785d-c699-4e97-962d-d4764ceba103?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) into hot music generation start-up Suno as [well as Udio](https://substack.com/redirect/01905e71-b3fc-4766-9694-6bf2960d79b0?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), arguing that their models had very likely been trained on large quantities of well-known copyrighted music. When provided with the lyrics of various well-known songs, Suno and Udio served up some eerily familiar chord sequences and melodies.

Meanwhile, Adobe is facing questions after it emerged that Firefly, its image-generating software, was [partially trained](https://substack.com/redirect/74a1248b-f3ea-444b-877c-084417865614?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) on images produced by Midjourney and other competitors. At first sight, this may not seem like a big deal. But Firefly‚Äôs big selling point was that it was safe from copyright disputes as it was primarily trained on Adobe‚Äôs own database of licensed images. While in practice this likely doesn‚Äôt hugely affect the commercial safety of Firefly, it‚Äôs non-ideal from a trust standpoint, especially as Adobe has been publicly critical of others‚Äô data collection practices.

Keen to avoid adding to lawyers‚Äô billable hours is OpenAI, with the company striking a [licensing deal with the FT](https://substack.com/redirect/e0af84de-f14e-4a11-99bb-7618d3391433?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI). The arrangement will allow OpenAI‚Äôs models to respond to questions with short summaries from FT articles and to link back to the source material, the fifth such arrangement the company has reached with a news publisher. Meanwhile, Reddit has warned that it will [consider suing](https://substack.com/redirect/54b0083d-1b76-4bd2-a275-2d6b1bbd8bc3?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) any company that uses its data for training purposes without authorization.

But if this kind of peaceful de-escalation doesn‚Äôt work, lawmakers may attempt to resolve these issues. Representative Adam Schiff has introduced the [Generative AI Copyright Disclosure Bill](https://substack.com/redirect/6510fd11-949e-44ef-a3bb-e7df6ecbcaee?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), with the backing of various artists and writers unions, which will compel anyone training a model to disclose any copyrighted material used in a public database. 

Whatever you think of copyright, the Bill as currently drafted is a case study in how not to navigate these questions. Considering how US copyright law works, it would likely require the disclosure of any works used to train an AI system, regardless of fair use considerations. Not only would this obliterate any commercial confidentiality, it would be prohibitively slow and expensive. While there may one day be a case for legislation on this issue, a loosely drafted five pages probably isn‚Äôt going to cut it. Having said all of this, it is likely that by the time legal action would run its course, various generative technologies and products will have diffused so broadly in the economy that it‚Äôll be too late to roll them back. Today, we‚Äôre basically in the pre-banning era of doping in competitive sports. Until bans are put in place, participants will juice themselves up as much as possible because everyone else is doing it. Ultimately, big companies will be in a position to pay potential fines, just like they‚Äôve done with GDPR, and move on.

### _**üç™ Hardware**_

Despite ever tightening US restrictions, high-end NVIDIA chips still have a habit of showing up in China. Tender documents [reveal](https://substack.com/redirect/54c519bb-b08b-4e74-a4f2-c17311d05d10?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) that Chinese government-linked entities have been acquiring NVIDIA chips by buying server products from Super Micro, Dell, and others via resellers. It‚Äôs not clear how or when these ended up in the hands of resellers, but it illustrates the challenge of having visibility into the downstream supply chain. Putting aside the China issue, it drives home just how challenging AI governance proposals [based on hardware control](https://substack.com/redirect/f4a4ea4a-f0ab-4dfd-b1dc-04bfdebb9d27?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) would be in practice.

That said, the Chinese government isn‚Äôt gambling on its ability to exploit loopholes in perpetuity. Officials in the Ministry of Industry and Information Technology [have directed](https://substack.com/redirect/756c97e3-f9ea-412b-9c10-665fe004bc84?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) the nation‚Äôs telecoms carriers to strip foreign processors out of their networks and switch to local alternatives, in a move that will primarily hit AMD and Intel. With China accounting for 28% of Intel‚Äôs revenue and 15% of AMD‚Äôs, it‚Äôs a reminder that the technology wars have the potential to spiral beyond AI and that there will be losers on both sides. Decoupling is both practically challenging and financially destructive. 

However, chipmakers have little choice. They‚Äôre reluctant players in the US government‚Äôs game of sanctions whackamole. Intel [is gearing up](https://substack.com/redirect/7bfec813-6119-46d2-b947-9703c5a6d15a?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) to release two new sanctions-compliant chips later this year, while NVIDIA has plans for another three. Considering US Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo has [already said that](https://substack.com/redirect/467e6afb-0e46-4f3c-a5ea-ebf5004ef840?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) _‚Äúif you redesign a chip around a particular cut line that enables them to do AI, I‚Äôm going to control it the very next day‚Äù_, it seems likely that many of these will go the same way as their predecessors.

Beyond US-China, the Indian government is [exploring the possibility](https://substack.com/redirect/7c1733f7-3d6c-4450-b765-8977d1adf111?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) of building a GPU cluster for start-ups, researchers, and academic institutions, with access offered at a subsidized rate. This follows on from the country‚Äôs decision in March to spend over $1 billion to buy 10,000 NVIDIA GPUs for use via public-private partnerships. 

While we commend the ambition, it‚Äôs always worth stopping to ask, as the Ada Lovelace Institute did recently: compute, [what is it good for](https://substack.com/redirect/f0e5da89-dda1-45e6-b7ef-41a875ee401f?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI)? The authors point out that spending big on a compute cluster is all well and good, but this needs to be accompanied by a sense of the intended public benefit and how this is likely to be realized. We‚Äôre already hearing reports of companies and investors acquiring GPUs before struggling to find a use for them. Governments need to be careful to avoid a cargo cult mentality in AI hardware.

However, if Mark Zuckerberg‚Äôs to be believed, none of this really matters. In his interview for the [Dwarkesh Podcast](https://substack.com/redirect/21ec5272-9b31-4fea-88c9-58bb091ca41a?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) to mark the launch of Llama 3 (more on that later), he talked about how Meta went on a GPU acquisition spree, essentially on the off-chance the company needed them, before generative AI was even on their radar. At the time, the company was attempting to optimize Reels and close the gap with TikTok, but deliberately overbought to create spare capacity. This proved to be a wise decision. But will it ultimately create a GPU supply overhang internally at Meta? 

In the same interview, Zuckerberg talked about how we‚Äôll one day move from a world of 50-100 MW data centers to 1GW - roughly equivalent to the size of a nuclear power plant. The scale of investment required to make this happen will be eye-watering and would ensure national AI leadership is strongly correlated with energy price. This would be good for the US, even better for China, and terrible for much of Europe.

### _**üè≠ Big ~~tech~~ start-ups**_ 

April finally saw the long anticipated launch of Meta‚Äôs Llama 3, with the company releasing the 8B and 70B models, with 400B+ models to follow later in the year. 

While the full paper will not be released for a few months, Meta disclosed that Llama 3 was trained on 15T tokens (up from 2T), using a similar architecture to Llama 2, with refinements to improve language encoding efficiency and the use of grouped query attention across both the 8B and 70B models. 15T tokens represents more data than is thought to have been used for GPT-4 and is absolutely massive for an 8B model. Meta themselves acknowledge that this is significantly in excess of the ‚ÄúChinchilla-optimal‚Äù amount of training compute, but say that both the 8B and 70B parameter models continued to improve log-linearly up to 15T. At a little over 8k, the context window remains shorter than most of its peers, but Meta says this will change in future releases.

The resulting model convincingly beat out Gemma, Claude 3 Sonnet, Gemma, and Gemini 1.0 Pro (and 70B Instruct against Gemini 1.5 Pro) across a range of benchmarks and it‚Äôs proving a hit in the community, as the highest-ranked open model on [the LMSYS leaderboard](https://substack.com/redirect/0b4f927e-c1d5-453f-8b79-f8773c88d3d6?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI). 

[![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/550x0,sFrZ9jHlTTLPJZ7fcAbDqSGN_SD9J2P6BJR43iHPXWZs/https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1276,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4854e17e-3ed8-40e2-abcf-5a2885afeebd_1600x900.png)](https://substack.com/redirect/dc071728-7256-4395-813f-16099e9b71d6?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI)

Meta paired the announcement with the launch of their new [Meta AI assistant](https://substack.com/redirect/5cd9118c-4b63-4541-b062-0b0bbaa84ba9?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), which they are billing as the most powerful freely available chatbot. It is also being integrated into WhatsApp, Messenger, or Instagram, but is currently geographically restricted. The integration already sparked a little controversy, with the standard litany of complaints about either [accuracy](https://substack.com/redirect/b553ab0b-1f9c-48be-940f-90821339b8f8?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) or [misinformation](https://substack.com/redirect/b5b2cb6d-6fda-4666-a762-a5f043eb988d?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI) quickly surfacing. However, as far as we can tell, people are yet to identify many Meta-specific gripes at this stage.

Llama 3 may have long been anticipated, but a few days later, a new entrant to the enterprise LLM wars unveiled itself: Snowflake. [Phi-3 Technical Report: A Highly Capable Language Model Locally on Your Phone](https://substack.com/redirect/f94177f3-8589-41b8-91c5-f91f42862989?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), _Microsoft_.

Introduces phi-3-mini, a 3.8B parameter language model that achieves performance rivaling much larger models like GPT-3.5 and Mixtral 8x7B, while being small enough to run on a smartphone. 

The authors specify that the model was deployed and tested on an iPhone 14 with an A16 Bionic chip. To enable on-device inference, the model was quantized to 4 bits, reducing its memory footprint to approximately 1.8GB. This quantized version of phi-3-mini was able to generate more than 12 tokens per second on the iPhone 14.

phi-3-mini was trained on 3.3 trillion tokens of heavily filtered web data and synthetic data, building upon the approach pioneered with the earlier phi-2 model. 

[![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/550x0,scYHcKsxiGKm6XIwA8BqtDTP9sSSeAr_7RDlSpfGR6Co/https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1252,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Feb0eec68-5a1f-4aaa-ad35-38a8eef714d2_1262x700.png)](https://substack.com/redirect/3e017833-3130-48c3-a73a-068419df3a30?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI)

While performing well on benchmarks testing reasoning and question-answering, its small size limited its ability on factual knowledge, compared to larger models. The authors also provide initial scaling results with 7B and 14B parameter models (phi-3-small and phi-3-medium) trained on even more data (4.8T tokens). These larger models exhibit further improvements in capability.

[Capabilities of Gemini Models in Medicine](https://substack.com/redirect/ba3aacf7-0b73-4c48-b9d1-42a3b1319f70?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), _Google DeepMind and Google Research._

Presents Med-Gemini, a new family of multimodal models for medicine, developed by fine-tuning Gemini Pro 1.0 and 1.5\. The researchers used a combination of instruction fine-tuning on medical datasets, self-training to improve complex reasoning, and integration with web search to access up-to-date information. 

[![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/550x0,smmo2agtciuv-916h_1q1QMTydkMOKcf6feX-VfOvmdI/https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1296,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64f23559-24d2-43de-a661-7fa0730458a4_1320x580.png)](https://substack.com/redirect/a3aa459a-29a6-46a1-bfe9-7285733b444f?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI)

For language tasks, they used datasets like MedQA, MIMIC-III clinical notes, and expert-written summaries to train Med-Gemini-L and Med-Gemini-M. For multimodal tasks spanning radiology, pathology, dermatology and other specialties, they fine-tuned Med-Gemini-M on datasets like MIMIC-CXR, Slake-VQA, Path-VQA and others. They also developed Med-Gemini-S with a custom encoder to process ECG waveforms.

On the MedQA benchmark of complex clinical reasoning, Med-Gemini-L achieved a new state-of-the-art accuracy of 91.1%, surpassing the prior best result of GPT-4 with specialized prompts. Careful relabeling of MedQA by clinicians revealed around 7% of questions had quality issues. Accounting for these, the model's performance is even higher. The strong reasoning capabilities generalized to other benchmarks like the New England Journal of Medicine (NEJM) diagnostic challenges.

For multimodal tasks, Med-Gemini established new state-of-the-art results on 5 out of 7 datasets evaluated, with an average 44% relative improvement over GPT-4.

Harnessing Gemini's long-context capabilities, Med-Gemini could process lengthy electronic health records (EHRs), achieving high precision and recall in retrieving rare findings - a challenging "needle-in-a-haystack" task. It also demonstrated strong results on medical video understanding, including surgical phase recognition and medical question answering.

Beyond benchmarks, Med-Gemini showed promising early results on tasks with real-world utility. These include generating high-quality clinical visit summaries, referral letters, and plain-language medical research synopses. In a preliminary study, clinicians found Med-Gemini's outputs to be on par with or better than human-written examples in most cases.

[The Open DAC 2023 Dataset and Challenges for Sorbent Discovery in Direct Air Capture](https://substack.com/redirect/c41965e3-8c6d-4481-bfa2-ca7a22fa074b?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI), _Meta and Georgia Institute of Technology_.

Introduces ODAC23, a collection of over 38 million quantum chemistry simulations, that‚Äôs built using physics-based computational chemistry methods. These simulations focus on how carbon dioxide and water molecules stick to the surfaces of more than 8,400 different porous materials known as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs). MOFs have long been thought to have potential applications in direct air capture. But due to the vast chemical space and the intricacies of understanding humidity and temperature, the discovery of promising MOF sorbents has proved challenging.

[![](https://proxy-prod.omnivore-image-cache.app/550x0,sbOlWZoo6YF_aopd7tN0QbaPZWretMNhA_xddu3dwRnw/https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/w_1236,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F64741976-0746-4295-abbe-49f2aeb0ec0a_1170x682.png)](https://substack.com/redirect/582ebb93-392c-4f12-bac7-15a63b7b9c19?j=eyJ1Ijoibm10MnYifQ.NjEarT2ZIO18IdxC9k5a0j0BJcwjvF6cgtuRD-MCySI)

The authors use the ODAC23 dataset to train a series of graph neural networks to predict the strength of adsorption and forces between the MOFs and gas molecules. Their best models offered comparable accuracy to traditional quantum chemistry calculations, while being significantly faster. The researchers were able to identify 241 MOFs of exceptionally high potential for direct air capture. The dataset and the baseline models have been released openly.
