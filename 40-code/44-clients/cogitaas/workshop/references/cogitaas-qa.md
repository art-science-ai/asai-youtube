- **What is AI in general terms?**
  - **TLDR (1-3 bullets)**
    - AI enables computers to perform tasks requiring human intelligence, evolving from rule-based systems to machine learning and deep learning.
    - It automates repetitive work, enhances productivity, and transforms industries like statistical consulting.
    - AI is pervasive but requires balancing innovation with risks like privacy and environmental impact.
  - **Detailed answer (3-5 bullets with more subbullets if needed)**
    - AI refers to computer systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as pattern recognition and decision-making.
      - It has been around for decades but modern AI (ML, deep learning) is more powerful due to advances in data and compute.
    - AI "eats the world" by transforming businesses from software to AI-powered applications, enabling new use cases.
      - Examples include personalization in ads and automating data analysis.
    - Challenges include energy consumption, ethical concerns, and the need for iterative development.
      - Myths abound, but AI is transformative, potentially more impactful than fire or electricity.
    - In corporate work, AI applies to predictive and prescriptive analytics, enhancing efficiency in fields like CPG marketing.
      - It complements human expertise rather than replacing it entirely.

    - s1e0.md: "AI is the new era of technology that we're in right now." (Nikhil Maddirala)

- **What is Gen AI in general? Is it useful for corporate work? Is it applicable in CPG companies? To what extent? What is Agent AI? How can we apply it?**
  - **TLDR (1-3 bullets)**
    - Gen AI generates new content mimicking training data, useful for ideation but prone to hallucinations in factual tasks.
    - Applicable in corporate work for content creation and insights; in CPG, for marketing materials and demand predictions.
    - Agent AI autonomously plans and executes tasks, applicable in automating data gathering and prescriptive recommendations.
  - **Detailed answer (3-5 bullets with more subbullets if needed)**
    - Gen AI creates new data (e.g., text, images) that resembles training data, powered by embeddings and transformers.
      - Examples include ChatGPT for text generation and Midjourney for images.
      - Useful for creative tasks like ideation and content creation in corporate settings.
    - In CPG companies, Gen AI applies to marketing materials, demand forecasting, and personalized campaigns, but limited by accuracy issues.
      - Extent: High for creative outputs, moderate for factual analytics due to hallucinations.
    - Agent AI refers to autonomous systems that break down tasks, access tools, and perform actions.
      - Examples: Jarvis or Apple Intelligence for multi-step workflows.
    - Application: In pricing scenario planning, agents can automate data gathering, analysis, and prescriptive recommendations.
      - Benefits: Scales complex tasks, but requires careful control for privacy and accuracy.
    - Overall, Gen AI and Agent AI complement corporate work by automating ideation and execution, fitting into iterative development processes.
  - **References**
    - s1e0.md: "Generative AI is just types of models that create new data that mimic characteristics of the training data." (Nikhil Maddirala)
    - s1e0.md: "Hallucination is the problem where when you ask an LLM question about something domain specific... it tends to hallucinate rather than admitting ignorance." (Nikhil Maddirala)
    - s1e0.md: "AI agents are based on having access to a knowledge base and then using that to perform, to get the relevant information and perform actions." (Nikhil Maddirala)

- **What AI is not. Is it a readymade tool which one can plug in? Or does it evolve iteratively within the org?**
  - **TLDR (1-3 bullets)**
    - AI is not a plug-and-play tool; it requires iterative development, prompt engineering, and integration.
    - Evolves through feedback loops, not instant deployment, involving trial-and-error in organizations.
    - Myths include instant autonomy; reality involves ongoing refinement and human oversight.
  - **Detailed answer (3-5 bullets with more subbullets if needed)**
    - AI is not a ready-made tool you can plug in without customization; it needs training on specific data and iterative tuning.
      - Requires understanding of models, prompts, and integration with existing systems.
    - It evolves iteratively within an organization through feedback loops, prompt engineering, and multi-agent systems.
      - Not a one-time setup; involves continuous improvement and adaptation to edge cases.
    - Myths: AI is not fully autonomous or job-replacing; it complements human expertise.
      - Debunking: Overhyped for short-term miracles; true progress comes from iterative development.
    - In practice, organizations must invest in POC (proof of concept) phases and ethical oversight.
      - Path: Start with basic tools, refine through use, and scale gradually.
    - Financially, iterative evolution leads to better ROI as models improve over time.
  - **References**
    - s1e0.md: "Chain of thought prompting is a way to improve the accuracy and output of the model." (Nikhil Maddirala)
    - s1e0.md: "You can always make things more and more powerful... with multi-step workflows." (Nikhil Maddirala)
    - s2e0.md: "We're in an AI bubble." (Referenced from external sources)
